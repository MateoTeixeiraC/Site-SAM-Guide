FAQ

SAM-Guide is a project funded by the ANR aimed at developing a mobility assistance device for blind and visually impaired people, combining tactile and auditory signals to facilitate navigation indoors and outdoors.

The project brings together several academic institutions:
  • University of Grenoble Alpes (UGA)
  • École Polytechnique / ENSTA Paris-Saclay
  • University of Rouen Normandie
  • University of Caen Normandie

  • 2022 : Launch of the project with the kick-off meeting at École Polytechnique.
  • 2023 : Progress on the development of the device, recruitment of researchers and engineers, experimental tests, and publications.
  • 2024 : Advances in the design of the belt with vibrators and integrated auditory signals.

  • Doctorante : Wenqi LUO, works on navigation through multimodal data fusion.
  • Study Engineers : Nicolas Charafe and Ilies Mohamed-Riad Zizi, recruited for their skills in signal processing, electronics, and programming.
  • Post-doctorant : Mathieu Thomas, specialized in ergonomics and cognitive analysis.
  • Other researchers and interns : Several Master's and Bachelor's students contributed to different phases of the project.

  • The HOMÈRE study, which analyzes the needs of blind and visually impaired people in France.
  • The VIP Platform, a central hub for resources on technology accessibility.
  • Outdoor localization systems, such as CENTIPEDE and GPS RTK ARDUSIMPLE.

The objective is to provide an innovative and effective tool that allows blind and visually impaired people to move around independently and safely, by leveraging advanced techniques in sensors, data fusion, and haptic feedback.
